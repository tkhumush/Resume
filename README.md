## Things I've Built
A sample of products I've designed, built, and shipped on my own.

**1. MempoolTV**  ·  *Swift, tvOS  ·  [App Store](https://github.com/tkhumush/MempoolTV)*
- **Why:** Bitcoin mempool data (fees, congestion, block activity) only lived in dense desktop dashboards, with no glanceable way to watch it on the biggest screen in the house.
- **What I built:** A native Apple TV app that streams live mempool insights to the living room. Now live on the App Store, on a platform most developers never touch.

**2. FastDiet: Last Meal Burner**  ·  *Swift, TypeScript  ·  [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/fastdiet-last-meal-burner/id1487273671)  ·  [PWA](https://github.com/tkhumush/FastDiet-PWA)*
- **Why:** Conventional fasting apps run on fixed time windows and ignore how much you actually ate.
- **What I built:** A fasting tracker that derives your eating window from "calories owed to your future self" rather than a static clock. Shipped as a native iOS app (live on the App Store) and re-platformed into an installable Progressive Web App for cross-platform reach.

**3. Marmot: Encrypted Messaging Stack**  ·  *Rust, TypeScript  ·  [CLI](https://github.com/tkhumush/marmot-cli)  ·  [plugin](https://github.com/tkhumush/openclaw-marmot)  ·  [ClawHub](https://clawhub.ai/plugins/@tkhumush/marmot)*
- **Why:** Secure group messaging usually means trusting a centralized server with your keys and metadata.
- **What I built:** An end-to-end encrypted messaging stack on the Marmot Protocol (MLS): a Rust CLI for key management plus a TypeScript channel plugin, published and approved on ClawHub, so every participant holds their own keys with no central authority.

**4. nostrTV**  ·  *Swift, Kotlin  ·  [tvOS](https://github.com/tkhumush/nostrTV)  ·  [Android TV](https://github.com/tkhumush/nostrTV-droid)*
- **Why:** Decentralized, open-protocol social feeds are built for phones, with no lean-back way to browse them on a big screen.
- **What I built:** A TV app that turns open-protocol video and feeds into a living-room experience, then took the same product across stacks to two platforms. The Swift/tvOS version is in beta on TestFlight pending iOS launch, with an Android TV / Chromecast port in Kotlin.

**5. BASIC Circassian**  ·  *TypeScript  ·  [repo](https://github.com/tkhumush/BASIC_Circassian)*
- **Why:** Circassian, an endangered heritage language, has almost no modern, approachable learning tools online.
- **What I built:** A clean, deployable web app that teaches the basics of the language, a personal-heritage project that doubles as a polished learning UI.

**6. Zaplan**  ·  *Svelte  ·  [repo](https://github.com/tkhumush/zaplan)*
- **Why:** To-do and planning apps lock your data into a single proprietary service.
- **What I built:** A to-do and project-planning web app on an open protocol with encrypted storage. My most-adopted open-source project, forked by other developers.

**7. Bitcoin District Relay**  ·  *self-hosted infrastructure  ·  [repo](https://github.com/tkhumush/BitcoinDistrictRelay)*
- **Why:** Communities relying on public infrastructure have no control over uptime, content, or media hosting.
- **What I built:** A self-hosted relay and media server so a local community can keep its traffic and media under its own control.

**8. Oneword**  ·  *TypeScript  ·  [repo](https://github.com/tkhumush/Oneword)*
- **Why:** Long-form reading on social-feed clients is cluttered and slow.
- **What I built:** A stripped-down reader focused on fast, distraction-free consumption of long-form notes.
